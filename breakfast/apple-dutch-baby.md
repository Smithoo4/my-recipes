# Apple Dutch Baby

A skillet-baked twist on the No-Fail Popovers — same foolproof batter with a touch of cinnamon, poured over caramelized apples and baked in a cast-iron pan until puffed and golden.

*breakfast, brunch, dessert, apple*

**4 servings**

---

- *191 grams* bread flour
- *3/4 teaspoon* table salt
- *1/2 teaspoon* cinnamon
- *1 1/2 cups* 2% low-fat milk, heated to 110 to 120 degrees (microwave until hot to the touch)
- *3* large eggs
- *2-3* apples, peeled and cut into 1/8-inch slices
- *1/4 cup* oil

---

1. Adjust oven rack to middle position and heat oven to 400 degrees (convection).
2. Whisk together flour, salt, and cinnamon in an 8-cup liquid measuring cup or medium bowl. Add milk and eggs and whisk until mostly smooth (some small lumps are OK).
3. Heat oil in a 10-inch cast-iron frying pan over medium-high until hot. Add apples and brown on one side, then flip.
4. Pour the batter over the apples (see note), then transfer the pan to the oven. Bake until lofty and deep golden brown all over, 40 to 45 minutes. Serve hot.

**Note:** When pouring the batter into the pan with the apples, do not pour directly into the center and let it spread out — that pushes all the oil to the edge of the pan. Instead, start about halfway between the center and the edge and pour in a slow spiral motion, trying not to display the oil.
